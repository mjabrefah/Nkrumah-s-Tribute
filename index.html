Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

Ghana's first president

Here's a time line of Dr. Nkrumah's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful in the Western Region, September 21st.
  • 1939 - Goes to the Unied States for advanced studies receiving a B.A. from Lincoln University.
  • 1942 - Receives an STB (Bachelor of Sacred Theology), a Master of Science in education from the University of Pennsylvania, and a Master of Arts in Philosophy the following year. Gets elected president of the African Students Organization of America and Canada.
  • 1945 - Helps to organise the Fith Pan-African Congress. Forms the West African National Secretariat to work for the decolonization of Africa. Serves as Vice-President of the West African Students' Union (WASU)
  • 1947 - Gets invitation to serve as the General Secretary to the United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC) under Dr Joseph Boakye Danquah. Takes up the position but split from it to form the Convention People's Party (CPP).
  • 1948 - Police fired on ex-servicemen protests the rising cost of living and shooting spurrs riots in Accra, Kumasi and elsewhere. Dr Nkrumah and other party leaders are suspected and arrested. The British soon releases the leaders. Emerges as the leader of the youth movement after his imprisonment.
  • 1949 - After his release, Dr Nkrumah went round the country proclaiming that the Gold Coast needed "self-government now", and built a large power base. Dr Nkrumah led the formation of the CPP at Arena in Accra before a crowd of some 60,000 on June 12 He was made Chairman, with Komla Agbeli Gbedemah as Vice Chairman and Kojo Botsio as Secretary. Other members of the Central Committee included N.A. Welbeck, Kwesi Plange, Krobo Edusei, Dzenkle Dzewu and Ashie Nokoi.
  • 1950 - Dr Nkrumah declares “positive action” on January 8 in front of a large CPP crowd at a public meeting in Accra. He travelles to Sekondi, Cape Coast and Takoradi to repeat it. The colonial government declares a state of emergency effective January 12, 1950 and prohibits the holding of processions, imposes curfews and disconnects public services in certain areas. Dr Nkrumah was arrested on January 21, tried for inciting an illegal strike and sedition for an article in the Cape Coast Daily Mail and sentenced to three years imprisonment at the James Fort prison from where messages were smuggled out on toilet paper to the party headquarters.
  • 1951 - While in prison, Dr Nkrumah leads the CPP to achieve a stunning victory in the February 1951 elections.
  • 1957 - He is freed to form a government, and he leads the colony to independence as Ghana.
  • 1963 - As a firm believer in African liberation, Nkrumah pursues a radical pan-African policy, playing a key role in the formation of the Organization of African Unity. At home, he led a massive socio-economic development that saw the springing up of infrastructure across the country. As time passes, he is being accused of being a dictator and also of forming a one-party state, with himself as president for life as well as actively promoting a cult of his own personality.
  • 1966 - Overthrown by the military, with the help of Western backing, he spends his last years in exile.
  • 1972 - Dies in Bucharest, Romania, on April 27. His legacy and dream of a "United States of African" still remains a goal among many.
